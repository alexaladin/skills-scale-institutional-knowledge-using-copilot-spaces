# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers own the user experience across the product. They create user flows, wireframes, and prototypes, and advocate for user-centric decision making throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Design user flows, wireframes, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct usability testing and synthesize user research
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design standards
- Collaborate with Product Managers to translate requirements into user-friendly designs
- Iterate on designs based on feedback from developers, stakeholders, and end-users

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Reduce friction and increase adoption through usability improvements
- Ensure design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with developers and Product Managers
- Usability test reports and research summaries
- Annotated wireframes and prototype walkthroughs

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- Works closely with **Developers** to ensure designs are implemented accurately
- Partners with **Product Managers** to align UX with product vision and acceptance criteria
- Coordinates with **Project Managers** on design milestones and sprint planning

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers maintain the delivery infrastructure that enables the team to ship reliably and quickly. They own CI/CD pipelines, automate deployments, and monitor system health.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines for automated testing and deployment
- Automate infrastructure provisioning and configuration management
- Monitor system health, performance, and availability
- Enable rapid iteration through reliable rollback and deployment mechanisms
- Manage environments (development, staging, production) and access controls

### Goals
- Maximize deployment frequency and minimize change failure rate
- Reduce mean time to recovery (MTTR) for incidents
- Ensure infrastructure is scalable, secure, and cost-efficient

### Typical Communication
- Incident postmortems and runbooks
- Pipeline and deployment status reports
- Infrastructure documentation and change logs

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- Collaborates with **Developers** on build tooling, test automation, and deployment pipelines
- Partners with **Security Engineers** to enforce infrastructure security policies
- Supports **Project Managers** by providing deployment readiness status and environment availability

---

## Customer Success

### Role Summary
Customer Success bridges the gap between end-users and the product team. They gather user feedback, monitor adoption metrics, and escalate customer pain points to drive product improvements.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and synthesize feedback from customers and end-users
- Monitor product adoption, usage trends, and customer satisfaction metrics
- Escalate recurring pain points or blockers to the Product Manager
- Support release communications and customer-facing announcements
- Assist with onboarding and training materials for new features

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and product adoption
- Reduce churn by proactively identifying and addressing pain points
- Ensure customers realize the full value of the product

### Typical Communication
- Customer feedback reports and escalation summaries
- Adoption and health dashboards shared with Product Managers
- Release notes and customer communication drafts

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- Feeds user insights to **Product Managers** to inform prioritization and roadmap decisions
- Coordinates with **Project Managers** on release timelines and customer impact
- Works with **Developers** to reproduce and clarify customer-reported issues

---

## Security Engineer

### Role Summary
Security Engineers protect the product and its users by identifying risks, reviewing code for vulnerabilities, and enforcing security policies throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and assess security risks during design, development, and deployment
- Conduct code reviews with a focus on security vulnerabilities
- Enforce security policies and compliance standards across the project
- Perform vulnerability assessments and penetration testing
- Serve as the escalation partner for security incidents and incident response

### Goals
- Minimize the attack surface and reduce security risk across the product
- Ensure compliance with relevant security standards and regulations
- Build a security-first culture within the engineering team

### Typical Communication
- Security review findings and remediation recommendations
- Threat models and risk assessments
- Incident response plans and postmortems

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- Reviews code and infrastructure changes with **Developers** and **DevOps Engineers**
- Advises **Project Managers** on security-related risks and dependencies
- Partners with **Product Managers** to incorporate security requirements into acceptance criteria

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts translate business goals and stakeholder needs into clear technical requirements. They document workflows, analyze business processes, and bridge the gap between business and engineering.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate business goals into actionable technical requirements and user stories
- Map and analyze existing workflows to identify process improvements
- Validate that implemented solutions meet business objectives
- Maintain requirements traceability and documentation

### Goals
- Ensure that technical work delivers measurable business value
- Reduce rework by clarifying requirements early in the project lifecycle
- Improve alignment and shared understanding between business and engineering teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and user story definitions
- Process flow diagrams and workflow analyses
- Stakeholder meeting notes and decision records

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- Collaborates with **Product Managers** to align business requirements with product vision
- Works with **Developers** to clarify acceptance criteria and resolve ambiguities
- Supports **Project Managers** in scope definition, risk identification, and dependency tracking

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts define success metrics, build dashboards, and analyze project outcomes to enable data-driven decisions. They ensure the team can measure impact and continuously improve.

### Responsibilities
- Define key performance indicators (KPIs) and signal metrics for projects
- Build and maintain dashboards to monitor project and product outcomes
- Conduct data analysis to surface insights on adoption, quality, and performance
- Support retrospectives with data-driven findings and trend analysis
- Identify data gaps and work with engineering to improve instrumentation

### Goals
- Enable data-driven prioritization and decision making
- Provide clear visibility into project outcomes and business impact
- Surface actionable insights that improve future project planning

### Typical Communication
- Metrics reports and outcome dashboards
- Analysis summaries presented at retrospectives and planning sessions
- Data instrumentation requirements shared with Developers

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- Partners with **Product Managers** to define and track success metrics
- Collaborates with **Project Managers** to measure delivery performance and surface risks
- Works with **Developers** to ensure proper event tracking and data instrumentation
